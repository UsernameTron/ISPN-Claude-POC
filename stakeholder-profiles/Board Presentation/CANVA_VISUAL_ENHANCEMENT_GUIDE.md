## General Canva Design Tips

### **Color Scheme Recommendations:**
- **Primary:** Professional blue (#0066CC or similar)
- **Accent:** Growth green (#00AA66 or similar)
- **Warning/Urgency:** Orange (#FF6600 for competitive urgency)
- **Background:** White or very light gray (#F5F5F5)
- **Text:** Dark gray (#333333) for readability

### **Icon Style:**
- Use consistent icon style throughout (line icons or filled icons, not mixed)
- Search for "line icon" or "outline icon" for clean, professional look
- Keep icons simple and recognizable

### **Typography:**
- **Headlines:** Bold, large (40-60pt)
- **Body Text:** Regular, readable (18-24pt)
- **Bullet Points:** Consistent formatting throughout
- **Key Numbers:** Make them stand out (bold, colored, larger)

### **Layout Principles:**
- **White Space:** Don't overcrowd slides - let visuals breathe
- **Hierarchy:** Most important information largest/boldest
- **Consistency:** Use same layout patterns for similar slides (all executive examples should look similar)
- **Alignment:** Keep elements aligned to grid for professional look

## Final Presentation Checklist

Before presenting to the board:

- [ ] All slides have consistent color scheme
- [ ] Icons are professional and consistent in style
- [ ] Key numbers (ROI, time savings) are visually prominent
- [ ] Complex concepts have simple visual explanations
- [ ] No slides are text-heavy (use visuals to break up content)
- [ ] Timeline/process flows show clear progression
- [ ] Before/after comparisons are visually striking
- [ ] Security/compliance visually reassuring
- [ ] Call-to-action (final slide) is clear and urgent
- [ ] Presentation tells a complete story from beginning to end
