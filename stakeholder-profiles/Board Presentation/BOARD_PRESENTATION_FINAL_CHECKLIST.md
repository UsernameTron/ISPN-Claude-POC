## Phase 5: Handling Board Questions

### Anticipated Questions & Answers:

**Q: "Is our data secure?"**
A: "Yes. Claude Desktop runs locally on ISPN machines. Data stays within our systems. We use existing permissions - if an executive can't access something, Claude can't either. Full audit trails maintained. Nothing leaves our environment."

**Q: "What if it makes a mistake?"**
A: "Claude generates outputs that executives review before distribution - same as any analyst's work. The accuracy is high, and the system learns from corrections. We also have validation checkpoints built into the 30-day pilot."

**Q: "How much does this actually cost?"**
A: "Total first-year investment is $28,500: $12,000 software, $8,500 setup, $8,000 training. Annual ongoing is about $15,000. Conservative ROI is 2,384% based on executive time value alone."

**Q: "What's the implementation risk?"**
A: "Very low. It's a 30-day phased pilot with clear go/no-go decisions at each week. If it doesn't deliver value, we stop. But based on what we've seen in testing, that's unlikely."

**Q: "Why now? Can't we wait?"**
A: "Two reasons: First, your competitors are exploring this now - delay means catching up instead of leading. Second, the ROI is immediate. Every month we wait is $58,000 in executive productivity we're not capturing."

**Q: "Can the whole organization use this?"**
A: "Yes, and that's the plan. We start with the 5-person executive team to prove value, then expand to their 10 direct reports, then broader. The unit economics improve as we scale."

**Q: "What about training? Is this complicated?"**
A: "Remarkably simple. If you can ask a question in English, you can use Claude. The 2-hour orientation in Week 1 is mostly about learning your system connections and setting preferences."

**Q: "How do we measure success?"**
A: "Four metrics: 85%+ daily executive usage, 40+ hours/week saved collectively, improved decision quality (measured by board feedback), zero security incidents. All measured in the 30-day pilot."

## Emergency Backup Plans

**If Presentation Tech Fails:**
- Have printed handouts of key slides (2, 11, 12, 15)
- Tell the story without slides - you know it well enough
- Offer to email full presentation and schedule follow-up

**If Questions Get Too Technical:**
- Acknowledge: "That's getting into implementation details..."
- Redirect: "The key business question is..."
- Defer: "Let's schedule a technical deep-dive with IT after this..."
- Focus: "What matters for this decision is..."

**If Board is Skeptical:**
- Offer immediate live demo on your laptop
- Propose 1-week CEO-only test before full pilot
- Show external validation (Anthropic customers, success stories)
- Focus on low-risk nature (30-day pilot, easy exit)

**If Time Runs Short:**
- Skip to slides 11 (urgency), 12 (ROI), 15 (decision)
- Hit these key points: Ready now, $697K return, 2-week payback, competitors moving
- Offer to send full presentation and schedule individual demos
